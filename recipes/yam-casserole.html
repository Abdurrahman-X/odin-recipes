Yam Casserole

Description

Delight your taste buds with a hearty and flavorful yam casserole, a dish that combines creamy textures with a perfect blend of sweet and savory flavors. Layers of tender yams are mashed or sliced and infused with warm spices like cinnamon and nutmeg, creating a rich, comforting base. A touch of butter and cream enhances the natural sweetness, while a hint of brown sugar or maple syrup takes it to the next level.

The casserole is topped with a crunchy pecan and breadcrumb mixture or gooey marshmallows, offering a delightful contrast to the creamy yams beneath. Baked to golden perfection, this dish emerges with an irresistible aroma and a caramelized crust. Perfect as a side for festive meals or as a cozy weeknight treat, yam casserole is a crowd-pleaser that brings warmth and satisfaction to every bite.

Ingredients

  • 1 (29 ounce) can yams, drained and mashed
  • ¼ cup melted butter
  • ½ cup white sugar
  • ½ cup evaporated milk
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • ½ teaspoon nutmeg
  • ½ teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ¼ cup butter, softened
  • ½ cup brown sugar
  • ½ cup chopped walnuts
  • 1 cup sugar frosted corn flake cereal

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
  2. In a large bowl combine yams, melted butter, white sugar, evaporated milk, eggs, nutmeg, cinnamon and vanilla extract. Mix well and transfer to a 9x9 inch casserole dish.
  3. In a medium bowl cream butter with brown sugar. Stir in walnuts and frosted cereal. Spread on top of yam mixture.
  4. Bake in preheated oven for 35 minutes.
